The Brief

Solutions In Me is a student innovation challenge run at FUPRE, sponsored by Aliva Tech and Gadgets. Students are challenged to identify real problems in campus and daily life, design practical solutions, and compete for a prize pool of ₦45,000. I served as volunteer graphic designer across all four phases of the campaign.

Design Challenge

The problem: A teaser post, an educational tip series, a crowd-building phase, and a submission announcement all need to feel like one campaign, yet serve completely different communication goals with very different information densities. Getting that right across 9 pieces produced over several weeks without the brand drifting is a real constraint.

Solution: The torn-edge navy banner motif. It runs through every single piece, creating a two-zone composition: primary messaging in the banner zone, supporting information in the white space above and below. The banner made every piece instantly recognisable as Solutions In Me content regardless of what type of flyer it was.

Brand System

Deep Navy #003366 as the dominant background. Authority, institutional seriousness, premium feel that separated this campaign from typical campus gradients and stock imagery. Bright Orange #FF6600 for the ME wordmark, numbered steps, section labels, and sponsor elements. The high contrast between navy and orange ensures key information lands even at thumbnail size in an Instagram grid.

DLCF FUPRE · Academic Unit Designer
DLCF Design System
The Brief

DLCF FUPRE needed consistent, well-designed social media content to keep students engaged academically, especially during high-pressure exam seasons. The audience: undergraduates who needed motivation without feeling lectured.

Design Challenge

Problem: Generic motivational posts get scrolled past. Students needed content that felt real, specific to campus life, and visually sharp enough to share. Approach: A recurring content system with four distinct visual formats, each with its own identity while remaining unmistakably the same brand.

The Work
Pieces

Stay Grounded Campaign: Bold typographic design with orange and navy identity, built for Instagram shareability during exam pressure periods.

Before-Class Checklist: Tilted notepad motif infographic, part of the recurring #Dailyacademictips series to encourage student preparation.

Academic FAQ Series: Conversational Q&A with yellow pill labels: non-preachy, answering real objections students use to justify skipping class.

Quote Series (Vol. 1 & 2): Frosted glass cards on study-desk backgrounds with keyword highlights. Instantly identifiable in a student's feed.

AeroPure Mini Launch Ad
The Brief

A smart air purifier needed a single-frame product ad that communicates features, quality, and urgency without overwhelming the viewer. Target format: Instagram and print.

Design Decisions

Freelance · Lagos Beauty Studio
Vina Beauté Brand Flyer
The Brief

A Victoria Island beauty studio needed a launch flyer that positions them as premium and aspirational while staying warm and inviting. Distribution channels: Instagram and WhatsApp.

Design Decisions

Palette: Warm gold and rose tones that communicate luxury without coldness. The warmth makes the brand approachable for a beauty audience while the gold creates the aspirational positioning the client needed. Typography: Bold serif display type for the brand name communicates premium quality immediately. Serifs in beauty branding signal heritage and confidence. Composition: High-impact model photograph occupying the majority of the frame. Frosted glass overlay for the service listing keeps it readable without obscuring the photography. The composition is built for a single scroll stop: see the image, read the name, know what they do.
